  • [9] Effect of lattice design on mechanical response of PolyJet additively manufactured cellular structures - The study investigates the effect of different design parameters, such as cell type and size, on the mechanical properties (elastic modulus and maximum stress) of additively manufactured polymer-based lattice structures, revealing that smaller cell sizes and tailored unit cells can enhance mechanical performance, enabling the creation of structures with either stiff or rubber-like behavior using the same material.
  • [10] Non-destructive microwave evaluation of TBC delamination induced by acute angle laser drilling - The paper explores the use of microwave non-destructive testing (NDT) to evaluate the integrity of yttria-stabilized zirconia thermal barrier coatings (TBCs) after acute-angle laser drilling, demonstrating the existence of delamination at the TBC/substrate interface, which was detected without the need for destructive sectioning.
  • [11] Mechanical properties of additively manufactured lattice structures designed by deep learning - The paper presents a novel approach using a deep learning-based Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) to design lattice structures with tailored mechanical properties, demonstrating that GAN-generated structures exhibit significantly improved strength and elongation performance, and showcasing AI's potential to enhance the design and fabrication of parts with optimized mechanical properties.
  • [12] Deep learning-enabled design for tailored mechanical properties of SLM-manufactured metallic lattice structures - The paper presents a new methodology using a deep learning-based 3D Generative Adversarial Network (3DGAN) model to design aluminum alloy lattice structures with tailored mechanical properties, demonstrating that the generated lattice designs exhibit significant improvements in energy absorption and extension capacities, and showcasing the potential of combining deep learning and Selective Laser Melting Additive Manufacturing (SLM-AM) for creating custom, high-performance parts.
  • [13] A systematic review of Inconel 939 alloy parts development via additive manufacturing process - The paper provides an overview of the advancements in additive manufacturing (AM) of IN939, a nickel-based superalloy, focusing on its processing via powder bed fusion techniques and electron beam powder bed fusion (EB-PBF), comparing results from different studies, and identifying research gaps to enhance the alloy's properties and applications in extreme environments.
  • [14] Investigation on selective laser-melted AlSi10Mg micro-struts: influence of processing parameters on dimensional accuracy, molten pool morphology and microhardness - The study investigates the effects of process parameters, strut diameter, and angle on the microstructure and mechanical properties of AlSi10Mg struts fabricated by selective laser melting (SLM), revealing that laser energy, power density, and strut inclination significantly influence densification, surface morphology, and mechanical performance, providing valuable insights for optimizing SLM processes in applications like medical science and space technologies.

Journal Impact Factor Quartile - Q2 Papers

  • [1] Nano-second pulsed DPSS Nd:YAG laser cutting of CFRP composites with mixed reactive and inert gases - The study investigates the thermal degradation characteristics of carbon-fibre-reinforced polymer composites during laser cutting using a nanosecond pulsed Nd:YAG laser, optimizing process parameters and improving quality by using low oxygen content assistant gas, which reduced fiber pull-out by 55% while maintaining a high processing rate.
  • [2] The effect of moisture content in fibre laser cutting of pine wood - The paper presents a statistical analysis of multiple-pass laser cutting of wet and dry pine wood using a 1 kW Ytterbium fibre laser, identifying key process parameters such as moisture content, cut direction, and laser power that influence cutting efficiency and quality, with comparisons to CO2 laser cutting.
  • [3] Understanding the process parameter interactions in multiple-pass ultra-narrow-gap laser welding of thick-section stainless steels - The paper investigates the significant process parameters and their interactions in ultra-narrow-gap laser welding of AISI 316L stainless steel using a 1 kW fiber laser, employing design of experiments and statistical modeling to optimize laser power, wire feed rate, and welding speed for improved weld quality, integrity, and bead shape.
  • [4] Statistical analysis of recast formation in laser drilled acute blind holes in CMSX-4 nickel superalloy - The study investigates the effects of pulse energy, pulse width, pulse frequency, and gas pressure on the metallurgy and drilling velocity of laser percussion drilled acute holes in CMSX-4 nickel superalloy, using statistical modeling to optimize drilling parameters for improved material removal rate and minimized recast layer thickness.
  • [5] Numerical investigation on laser stripping of thermal barrier coating - The paper develops a three-dimensional finite element model to simulate the transient temperature field, residual thermal stress, and material removal during the laser stripping of thermal barrier coatings (TBC), providing insights into the mechanisms of material ablation and thermal damage, and validating the model with experimental results using a nanosecond pulsed laser.
  • [6] Dual gas jet-assisted fibre laser blind cutting of dry pine wood by statistical modelling - The paper presents a statistical analysis of the performance of an Ytterbium fibre laser using single and dual gas jets to blind cut dry pine wood, employing design of experiments and statistical modeling to optimize process parameters and achieve high cutting efficiency and quality, with results showing that dual gas jets produce superior surface finishes compared to a single jet.
  • [7] Single and dual gas jet effect in Ytterbium-doped fibre laser cutting of dry pine wood - The paper investigates the effects of single and dual assist gas jets in the laser cutting of dry pine wood, using a three-dimensional computational fluid dynamic model to analyze gas jet roles in fume and combusted material removal, and finding that shear stresses generated by the gas jets significantly influence the yield and cut quality.
  • [8] Performance of coated and uncoated carbide/cermet cutting tools during turning - The paper investigates the performance improvements of Al2O3 and TiN coated cutting tools in turning operations, analyzing the effects of process parameters like cutting speed, feed rate, and cutting depth through 540 experiments, and using an artificial neural network model to predict optimum performance parameters based on in-situ cutting force measurements and surface roughness.
  • [9] A Review on Laser Beam Shaping Application in Laser-Powder Bed Fusion - The paper reviews laser beam shaping techniques and their application in laser-powder bed fusion (L-PBF) for metallic component production, examining how different beam profiles influence melt pool geometry, process stability, productivity, mechanical properties, microstructure, and surface roughness, while identifying challenges, constraints, and future directions for optimizing L-PBF processes.
  • [10] Synthesis of Aluminium Nitride-Based Coatings on Mild Steel Substrates Utilising an Integrated Laser/Sol–Gel Method - The study introduces an integrated laser/sol–gel method for synthesizing aluminium nitride (AlN) coatings on EN43 mild steel substrates, overcoming the limitations of traditional coating methods by achieving strong metallurgical bonding, high hardness (876 HV), low friction (≤0.1), and excellent wear resistance, with coatings demonstrating a thickness of 4-5 µm and surface roughness between 1.8 to 2.1 µm.

Journal Impact Factor Quartile - Q3 Papers

  • [1] Mechanisms of Acute Angle Laser Drilling Induced Thermal Barrier Coating Delamination - The study investigates the mechanisms of delamination in laser-drilled acute-angled holes in ceramic thermal barrier coatings (TBCs), identifying melt ejection-induced stresses as the primary cause of delamination at the coating/coating interface, while thermal effects dominate at the bond coating/substrate interface.
  • [2] Additive manufacturing of carbon fiber reinforced plastic composites by fused deposition modelling: effect of fiber content and process parameters on mechanical properties - The paper investigates the feasibility of 3D printing with 6 mm long carbon fiber reinforced ABS composite filaments using Fused Deposition Modelling (FDM), demonstrating significant improvements in part strength with increased carbon fiber content, while also highlighting the impact of print pattern on the mechanical properties and the diminishing flexibility and processability with higher reinforcement content.
  • [3] Recast and oxide formation in laser-drilled acute holes in CMSX-4 nickel single-crystal superalloy - The study investigates the effects of four controllable variables—assist gas pressure, nozzle stand-off distance, focal plane position (FPP), and number of pulses—on the metallurgy and geometry of laser percussion drilled holes in CMSX-4 nickel superalloy, using design of experiments and statistical modeling to optimize process parameters for minimal oxide and recast layer thickness.
  • [4] An investigation into delamination mechanisms in inclined laser drilling of thermal barrier coated aerospace superalloys - The article investigates the role of melt ejection and the coaxial assist gas jet in low-angle laser drilling of thermal barrier-coated substrates, using a finite volume method to reveal that melt ejection and accelerated molten metal flow at the leading edge of the hole significantly contribute to crack formation and delamination at the coating/substrate interface.
  • [5] Statistical analysis of ytterbium-doped fibre laser cutting of dry pine wood - The paper presents a statistical analysis of the laser cutting process of dry pine wood, using design of experiments and statistical modeling to investigate the impact of process parameters such as laser power, cutting speed, and gas pressure on process efficiency and cut quality, highlighting the significant effect of wood structure on the results.

  • [2] The design process of the complex Special Utility Vehicle using the methodical design approach - The paper discusses the design and manufacturing process of a complex special utility vehicle (SUV) using the methodical design approach established by Pahl & Beitz, sharing practical insights and solutions to challenges encountered, particularly for small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs), based on over 25 years of design research and practice.

  • [4] The Effect of 3D-Printed Custom Insoles on Plantar Pressure for Obese Individual - The study investigates the effectiveness of 3D-printed insoles made from flexible TPU-75A filament at different fill rates (5%, 10%, and 15%) in reducing plantar pressure in obese individuals, finding that 3D-printed insoles distribute pressure more effectively and reduce foot pain compared to traditional insoles, offering a personalized, labor-efficient alternative.
